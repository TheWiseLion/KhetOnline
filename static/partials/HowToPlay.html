How to play Khet

Khet is a chess like variant where pieces are taken when hit by a laser on any of its flat sides

The game ends when a players pharaoh is taken

Pieces

Sphinx

  • Each player has one sphinx piece
  • The sphinx produces laser light in the direction it's oriented
  • The sphinx cannot move to a different square and has two possible orientations
  • The sphinx cannot be taken even if hit by a laser

Anubis

  • The piece can move to any unoccupied adjacent square
  • The piece is not taken when the laser hits the patterned side of the piece

Pharaoh

  • The piece can move to any unoccupied adjacent square
  • The player loses if this piece is taken (equivalent to the king in chess)

Pyramid

  • The piece can move to any unoccupied adjacent square and can rotate
  • Light bounces 90° if hit on the hypotenuse of the triangle

Scarab

  • The piece can move to any unoccupied adjacent square and can rotate
  • The piece can swap spaces with any adjacent pyramid or anubis pieces
  • Light bounces 90° if hit


Taking a turn

  • During a turn you can move a piece or rotate it 90° (for those pieces that rotate)
  • At the end of the turn the moving colors sphinx will apply it's laser effect taking up to one piece based on the rules above
  • Note: This also means you can take your own pieces!
